You will analyze whole-genome sequencing (WGS) data from A549, a lung Adenocarcinoma cell line.

Analysis of WGS of the A549 cell line

For this final assignment, we will analyze the A549 cell line, especifically chromosomes 9 and 12.

The final goal of the assignment is to identify genetic variants (SNPs, Indels and SVs) driving the cancer of this cell line.

The bioinformatic pipeline will be based on a reference-based approach, using the GRCh38 human reference genome.

Task 1: Bioinformatic pipeline

Note
  1. List the files of the input FastQ folder. Of the sequencing technologies reviewed in this course, what’s the most likely technology used for this dataset? Why? (5pts)
  2. Outline the steps of your suggested pipeline to identify and annotate SNPs and SVs of clinical significance and name the bioinformatic software used for each step (15pts)
  3. Attach a SLURM job with the entire pipeline (10pts)

Task 2: SNPs and Indels

Note

Find the following fields in either the QUAL, INFO or the FORMAT columns and filter with the specified value. - QUAL: Minumum of 20 - Average mapping quality: Minimum of 20 - Raw read depth: Minimum of 20 - Allelic depths (high-quality bases): Minimum of 10

  1. Paste your filtering command (5pts)
  2. How many high confident SNPs did you identify? How many high confident Indels? (5pts)
  3. Are all these variants causing the cancer? Explain your answer (5pts)
  4. Annotate your SNPs and Indels using the GnomAD and ClinVar databases. Suggest filters using the information from these databases. Why did you choose those filters? (10pts)
  5. Given your annotations and filters, what are the SNPs/Indels driving this cancer? Search for this position in ClinVar. Report the Gene Name and the genomic coordinates. (10pts)

Task 3: SVs

Note

Find the following fields in either the INFO or the FORMAT columns and filter with the specified value. - Median mapping quality of paired-ends: Minumum of 20 - Paired-end support of the structural variant: Minimum of 10 - Split-read support: Minimum of 10 - High-quality variant pairs: Minimum of 20

  1. Paste your filtering command (5pts)
  2. Annotate your high confident SVs using VEP. Extract the name of the gene where the SV falls in. Find information about the identified genes. Which gene or genes are most likely to be linked to cancer? (10pts)
